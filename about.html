Welcome

Hi, I’m Pravesh.

I am a researcher working in computer architecture, with a particular focus on virtual memory and address translation. Much of my academic curiosity revolves around how modern systems move, translate, and manage data efficiently beneath the surface of computation.

This blog is a space where I hope to document not only my research journey, but also the many ideas, questions, and experiences that shape the way I think.

Outside research, I enjoy cycling, running, trekking, and occasionally playing chess. I also have a deep interest in stories, especially the kind that build entire worlds and stay with us long after they end. Works like Attack on Titan, Harry Potter, and Pirates of the Caribbean have inspired my desire to write, even though I am still at the very beginning of that path.

I am also drawn to philosophy, mindfulness, consciousness, and meditation. I often find myself wondering why some truths seem impossible to fully capture in language. Certain ideas, especially those related to awareness, presence, and inner experience, appear to be understood only when lived directly. Words can point toward them, but they rarely seem to contain them.

This blog is my attempt to explore both worlds: the technical and the personal, the logical and the experiential, the architecture of machines and the architecture of the mind.

Welcome to my space.
